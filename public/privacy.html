Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 12, 2026

Overview

This policy explains how Big Bear Engineering GmbH handles personal data collected through this website. It is written for a business-to-business website used to describe services and receive inbound contact requests.

What data we collect

  • Information you submit through the contact form, such as name, work email, company, request type, and message.
  • Technical request data normally generated by web hosting and content delivery systems, such as IP address, browser metadata, and request timestamps.
  • Consent preference data stored in your browser when you dismiss the cookie notice.

How we use the data

  • To review and respond to inbound business requests.
  • To operate, secure, and troubleshoot the website.
  • To maintain an internal lead and inquiry record.
  • To comply with legal obligations and protect our systems and rights.

Contact form processing

When you submit the website contact form, the page is designed to forward the request into a Google Sheets-based intake workflow operated by Big Bear Engineering. That workflow may include Google Apps Script and Google Workspace services. Please do not submit secrets, credentials, or special-category personal data through the website form.

Legal bases

Where applicable, processing is based on consent, pre-contractual business communication, legitimate interests in operating and securing the website, and compliance with legal obligations.

Sharing

We may share limited data with service providers that help us host the website, protect it, or operate the contact-intake workflow. We do not sell personal data submitted through this site.

Retention

We retain submitted contact requests for as long as reasonably necessary to manage the conversation, evaluate commercial opportunities, maintain records, and meet legal or operational requirements.

Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, or portability of your personal data. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

International processing

Some service providers used for hosting or form processing may process data outside Switzerland or the European Economic Area. Where relevant, transfers should be covered by appropriate contractual or organizational safeguards.

Updates

We may update this policy as the website, tooling, or legal requirements change. Material changes should be reflected by updating the date at the top of this page.

Important note

This is an operational policy draft for the current site setup, not legal advice. It should be reviewed and finalized with counsel before being treated as production-ready legal text.
